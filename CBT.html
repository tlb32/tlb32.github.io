Clara Brooks Wins Nassau Championship After Harrowing Skate-Saving Dash

From Connecticut to Championship Gold: Skater Overcomes Pre-Competition Crisis

By Amelia Hayes
May 17, 2025

NASSAU COUNTY, NY — Clara Brooks of Connecticut delivered a stunning performance at the Nassau County Figure Skating Championships on Friday, claiming first place despite a chaotic start to her day that nearly derailed her competition entirely. Brooks, 11, electrified the audience with a dynamic and energetic program set to “Bop to the Top,” earning her highest competition score to date.

What the spectators didn't know was the drama unfolding behind the scenes. Roughly halfway to the Long Island rink, Brooks and her family realized a critical error: her ice skates remained at home in Connecticut.

“It was a moment of sheer panic,” said a family member. “We were already battling heavy Friday traffic, and the realization that Clara’s skates weren’t with us felt like a disaster.”

A frantic decision was made to turn around and retrieve the skates. The drive, already lengthy, became a race against the clock. Fortunately, they arrived at the rink just as Brooks’ group began their warm-up session. With a mere 30 seconds to prepare, Brooks took to the ice, managing a quick lap and a few jumps, including a flawlessly executed double salchow, to shake off the stress and regain her focus.

“She was visibly fueled by adrenaline,” observed one judge. “There was a nervous energy, but it translated into a performance full of speed and dynamism. It was truly captivating.”

Brooks’ program was a highlight of the competition, brimming with impressive spins, agile footwork, and powerfully landed jumps. The judges rewarded her with a personal best score, cementing her place at the top of the podium.

Following her victory, the Brooks family celebrated with a well-deserved dinner at a local Indian restaurant before beginning the drive back to Connecticut. The celebration was a little more relaxed, knowing that Clara had overcome a significant hurdle to achieve her goal.

The family will be back at the rink tomorrow as Clara’s sister, Lucy, is scheduled to compete. They're hoping for a smoother, less stressful day.
